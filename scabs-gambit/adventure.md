# SCAB'S GAMBIT

### A Filthy Few Adventure for 3-6 Level 3 Goblin Characters

**Set in the Blackstone Sewers**

---

## ADVENTURE SUMMARY

The goblin gangs of the Blackstone sewers have always fought amongst themselves - scrappy, petty wars over tunnel junctions and drainage rights, settled with rusty shivs and bad language. But the balance of power has shifted.

Two weeks ago, the **Grimebacks** - a mid-tier gang operating out of the old waterworks district - ambushed a group of adventurers who had descended into the sewers to clear out a nest of mutant rats. The adventurers were already wounded and exhausted from the extermination job. The Grimebacks caught them resting, swarmed them, and killed them to the last.

The magic items the adventurers carried were rare and powerful. Armed with these artifacts, the Grimebacks have become a terror. Rival gangs have lost entire crews in skirmishes. The **Snotrocket Society**, the dominant gang in the central sewers under the recently crowned **King Scab**, has felt the pressure most of all. Three Snotrocket scouting parties have been wiped out in the last week.

Now, word has reached Scab that the Grimebacks' boss, **Mogrot the Wide**, is planning a full-scale raid on Snotrocket territory. If the raid happens with those magic items in play, the Snotrockets could be finished.

The players take on the roles of goblins in the **Filthy Few**, a notorious crew of Snotrocket operatives tasked by King Scab to infiltrate the Grimeback stronghold - a fortified defunct pumping station called **the Dripfort** - and steal the magic items before they can be used to destroy the Snotrocket Society.

The Filthy Few's raid on the Dripfort is a sandbox stealth mission that encourages the players to use espionage, stealth, trickery, and goblin cunning to survive and escape with the artifacts. Head-on combat against the Grimebacks in their own territory is likely suicide.

---

## STARTING THE ADVENTURE

This adventure is designed for 3-6 players. The adventurers will play as the Filthy Few, an established crew of Snotrocket goblins known for pulling off dangerous jobs in the Blackstone sewers. Character sheets for each member of this crew are located at the back of this book. Each player should control one character. Since this is a stealth mission, completing the adventure with three players is possible. In fact, some sneaking sections may be easier, while head-on battles are much more difficult.

If your players prefer to create their own characters, they should be goblins (or goblinoids) with ties to the Blackstone sewers. Player Characters should be level 3 at the start of this adventure.

All creature and NPC statistics are provided in the Bestiary section of the book. When encountering a creature during the adventure, we **bold** the text to indicate that the creature's stat block can be found at the back of the book.

Additionally, all magic items in this adventure can be found in Appendix A at the back of the book.

## BEEFS & BONDS

The Filthy Few have history. They've run jobs together in the past and survived with the odds stacked against them, leaving them with some mental scars. Each member of the crew has a **Beef** with one other member and a **Bond** with another.

Allow each player to pick something that happened in the past to represent a Beef with one other character. The Beef could be an old grudge, an event that soured the relationship, or maybe something about the other character that just irks them. You can randomly assign Beefs by going around the table and rolling a die based on the number of players.

## THE SNOT PIT

The Snot Pit is the massive central basin from which all of Blackstone's sewers branch and wind outward beneath the city. Rising from the filth at its center is a great island of wreckage and refuse, upon which the Snotrocket Society has built its stronghold - a crooked, sprawling town of scrap and stolen timber, tethered to the basin's outer rim by sagging rope bridges. Roughly two hundred Snotrockets live here. Around the island's edges, the Snotrocket navy bobs in the dark water - a ramshackle fleet of stolen wooden washtubs fitted with crude catapult launchers and barrel rafts lashed together from old Ratpiss Reserve casks. They patrol the basin, fish for drain fish, and stand ready to lob bewilderment bombs at anything unfriendly that approaches the island.

Above it all, rising from the highest point of the island, stands the Pole of Stinky - King Scab's ceremonial flagpole, with his beloved snotrag Stinky flapping brown and holy at its peak. Its putrid, sacred aroma fills the entire basin. No matter where you are in the Snot Pit - sleeping in the bunks, haggling at the scrap market, eating Toadphlegm Soup in the kitchens - you can smell Stinky. To the Snotrockets, that smell is home.

Players are free to explore the Snot Pit before departing on their mission. Key locations include King Scab's war tent (atop the highest heap of trash in the town center), the Bunks (sleeping quarters), the Brew Pipe (kitchen and brewery, run by Old Gusset), the Scrap Market (a trading bazaar), and the Stink Shrine (maintained by Nosebleed Peg, the self-appointed priestess of Stinky). Full details for each location can be found in **Appendix S: The Snot Pit**.

## MEETING THE BOSS

> *Scab has a Napoleon complex. He is small, even for a goblin, but speaks as though he's addressing subjects from a balcony. He over-enunciates. He pauses for effect. He refers to himself as "your king" more than is strictly necessary. When he feels disrespected, he sniffs loudly and deliberately.*

> You have been summoned by King Scab. Whatever you were doing, you stop doing it. When the king calls, you go.
>
> You make your way through the winding paths of the Snot Pit - squeezing between shacks, stepping over a goblin passed out in a puddle of Ratpiss Reserve, ducking under a clothesline strung with rat pelts. Near the center of the island, the ground rises. The trash is packed hard here, piled into a steep mound that towers over everything else. At its peak sits a war tent of stitched rat hides, and rising above it, the Pole of Stinky - the Snotrocket banner, the holiest object in all the sewers. The brown rag flaps lazily in the draft, and the closer you climb, the harder it hits. That thick, sacred, eye-watering stench that every Snotrocket knows in their bones. Some of you breathe deeper out of reverence. Some of you because you genuinely enjoy it.
>
> You push through the tent flap and find King Scab seated upon his massive throne - a brutish thing of welded scrap and jagged metal, clearly built for someone much, much bigger than the goblin currently sitting in it. Crowned in his moldy old leather boot, Scab's legs dangle a full foot off the ground. The king turns to you.
>
> "My Filthy Few. How good of you to come so quickly to your king. I have a job for you. The kind where I send goblins I trust and hope they come back. You should feel honored. You should also feel nervous. Both are appropriate."
>
> He hops down from the throne - it takes him a moment to land properly - and walks toward you, the cleaver dragging on the ground behind him.
>
> "Two weeks ago, a pack of topsider adventurers came down into the western tunnels to clear out a nest of mutant rats. Dangerous lot - magic weapons, magic armor, the whole bit. But the rats wore them down, and the Grimeback goblins stumbled on them while they were licking their wounds. Got lucky. Swarmed them. Killed every last one. And now Mogrot the Wide - and believe me, the name is generous - is sitting in his little fortress playing dress-up with three very dangerous magic items that he is far too stupid to understand."
>
> He stops. Turns. Faces you.
>
> "He's already wiped out three of my scouting parties with those items. Three. And my spy inside says he's planning a full raid. On us. On the Snot Pit. On everything your king has built."
>
> He lets that settle. Then he smiles.
>
> "So you are going to fix it. You are going to sneak into his stronghold - an old pumping station the Grimebacks have fortified. They call it the Dripfort, because Grimebacks have no imagination. You are going to steal those three items right out from under his fat nose. Quietly. Cleverly. The way Snotrockets do things."
>
> He climbs back into the throne. It takes two attempts.
>
> "Questions?"

Scab's three advisors stand behind the throne: **Gumwort** (a nervous, twitchy goblin who flinches at loud noises), **Spackle** (a slow, deliberate speaker who weighs every word), and **Damp Steve** (who never speaks - he only nods or shakes his head; nobody knows why).

When the Filthy Few have questions, Scab fields a few himself before waving at his advisors to handle the rest.

> *Gumwort speaks in a nervous, twitchy voice.*
> *Spackle speaks slowly and deliberately, like every word costs him effort.*
> *Damp Steve communicates exclusively through nods, head shakes, and meaningful stares.*

**Q: What items were stolen?**
A magic spear that melts everything around it when it hits. Living armor made of rats. And a cloak that makes you vanish.

**Q: Who has them?**
Spackle answers: **Rattooth**, Mogrot's enforcer, carries the spear. **Skivvy**, their scout captain, wears the rat armor. **Blort**, their shaman, has the cloak. All three have been used against Snotrocket scouts in the last two weeks.

**Q: Where is the Dripfort?**
West through the Blackflow - the old industrial tunnels. Two hours on foot. Dangerous territory.

**Q: How many Grimebacks are there?**
Roughly sixty. Maybe more.

**Q: Do we have anyone inside?**
A Snotrocket goblin named **Drizzle** got herself hired into the Grimeback kitchens about a month ago. She's been feeding intel back via trained sewer rats. Her last message included a map of the Dripfort. Damp Steve hands the Filthy Few the water-stained map. He nods once. (See Drizzle's Dripfort Map, Player Handout.)

**Q: When is the raid?**
Three days. Maybe sooner. Mogrot is not a patient goblin.

**DM Note: Essential Information.** Not every group of players will think to ask the right questions. If the Filthy Few are about to leave without asking, Scab or his advisors should volunteer the following before they go. These are critical to the mission:

- **The three items and who holds them** (Rattooth, Skivvy, Blort)
- **Drizzle is inside the Dripfort** and can help once they arrive
- **They have three days** before the raid launches
- **The Dripfort is surrounded by a toxic sludge moat** with one guarded bridge
- **Gunk and Fizzpop** can help them get across the moat

Scab is arrogant, not careless. He wouldn't send his crew into a job blind. If they don't ask, he tells them anyway - impatiently, as though they should have already known.

**Q: How do we get in?**
The Dripfort is surrounded by a moat of toxic sludge. One guarded bridge. Scab leans back and gestures at his advisors:

> "Getting in is your problem to solve, but I'll point you in the right direction."

- "Go talk to **Gunk**. Troll. Lives under the bridge at the west end of our territory. Stupid but strong. He can get you across that moat if you make it worth his while." (See **Recruiting Gunk** below.)
- "Or go see **Fizzpop** in the Eastern Pipes. She's insane, but she builds things that work. Sometimes. Go ring her bell." (See **Speaking with Fizzpop** below.)

**Q: How do we find Drizzle?**
She's in the kitchens. She'll find you once you're inside.

**Q: How do we escape?**
Talk to Drizzle. She has a plan.

**Q: Can we disguise ourselves as Grimebacks?**
Spackle frowns. The Grimebacks mark themselves with a streak of grey clay across their foreheads. Faking the mark is easy, but Grimebacks know each other by sight and smell. Disguising a Snotrocket as a Grimeback would require a disguise kit, a solid Performance check, and a lot of luck.

Before the characters leave, the advisors drag a battered footlocker out from behind the throne.

### Snotrocket Footlocker

- 2x bags of caltrops
- 2x 10-foot lengths of chain
- A crowbar
- A grappling hook
- A hunting trap
- A pack of 10 torches
- 50 feet of silk rope

- A shovel
- An acid vial
- An alchemist's fire

As the goblins finish looting, Scab calls out one final time. His voice drops low:

> "One more thing. Drizzle's last message mentioned something. Mogrot keeps a big copper pot in his quarters. Sealed shut. She doesn't know what's in it and neither do I, but your king's gut says leave it alone. Don't open it. Don't touch it. Don't even look at it too long. You hear me?"
>
> "Get in. Get the items. Get out. That's it."

If the adventurers press for more, Scab waves them off. The advisors only advise that they trust the king's instincts.

## RECRUITING GUNK

> *Gunk speaks in a deep, slow rumble. He often forgets what he was saying mid-sentence and starts over.*

> The tunnel narrows to a single-file passage before opening into a wide junction where a crude stone bridge spans a twenty-foot gap over a sluggish grey river. Underneath the bridge, wedged into a space far too small for his body, sits a troll. He's enormous - easily nine feet tall - and covered in a permanent layer of green-grey slime. Patches of moss and small mushrooms grow in the folds of his skin. He's gnawing on what appears to be a bicycle wheel.
>
> He looks up as you approach and squints.
>
> "Hmm. Little goblins. Gunk was eating. What you want?"

When the Filthy Few explain the mission to Gunk, he scratches his head for a long time. He agrees to help for the price of **one whole surface rat** - not a sewer rat, a proper fat one from above. He's very specific. He describes the taste in uncomfortable detail.

A DC 16 Persuasion check convinces him to come along for the good of his alliance with the Snotrockets. (They bring him a rat every month. If they die, no more rats.)

A surface rat can be acquired in the upper tunnels near the street grates for 5 silver pieces from a goblin rat-catcher. The price can be haggled down to 3 silver with a DC 12 Persuasion check. Scab will also provide one from the royal stash if the Filthy Few return to ask.

> *Stealing a rat from a rat-catcher is also possible. It's a DC 15 Stealth check to swipe one from a cage. If the players devise a clever distraction, this check is made with advantage. If caught, the rat-catcher attacks the thief with a club. Use the Snotrocket goblin stat block for any combat that may occur.*

Once payment is made or promised, Gunk will travel to the Dripfort on his own and wait near the southern edge of the sludge moat.

> "Gunk throw good. Real good. You fly over bad water. Maybe land okay. Maybe land... less okay. But you get across. Gunk guarantee. Mostly."

## SPEAKING WITH FIZZPOP

> *Fizzpop speaks at approximately twice the speed of a normal goblin. She rarely finishes a sentence before starting a new one.*

> You navigate down a narrow service tunnel, squeezing past corroded pipes and tangles of wire. At the dead end, a rusty metal door is covered in scratched warnings: "KEEP OUT," "DANGER," "DO NOT KNOCK (ring bell)," and in smaller letters - "Seriously, don't knock. Last goblin who knocked lost a finger." A small bell hangs from a string beside the door.
>
> You ring the bell. Silence. Then a series of clicks, whirrs, and one small explosion later, the door swings open. A wild-eyed goblin with singed eyebrows and goggles made from bottle bottoms peers up at you from behind a wall of salvaged junk.
>
> "Yes? What? Come in? No, don't come in. Actually yes, come in. But don't touch anything. Especially not the green thing. Which green thing? All of them."

Fizzpop hears the predicament and immediately begins rummaging through her piles of salvage.

> "The sludge moat, yes yes yes, I know about it. Very toxic. Very deadly. Very not-good for swimming. But! I have - where is it - AH HA!"
>
> She drags out what appears to be a large barrel cut in half, fitted with crude paddles and a bellows system.
>
> "The Scumskimmer! Floats on anything. Sludge, acid, doesn't matter. Coated in rendered troll fat. Nothing sticks. Fits three goblins per trip. Quiet as a dead rat if you paddle slow. Loud as a live rat if you paddle fast. Don't paddle fast."

Fizzpop asks for the following items as payment:

- The grappling hook
- 25 feet of silk rope
- The acid vial

Fizzpop can be bargained with - perhaps half the silk rope, or the removal of one item. Small requests require a DC 12 Persuasion check. To arrange for a full deferment of payment, a DC 18 Persuasion check is required.

Once the deal is struck, Fizzpop will haul the Scumskimmer to the edge of the sludge moat on her own and meet the Filthy Few there.

## THE TICKING CLOCK

As Scab warns, the Grimeback raid is fast approaching. If the Filthy Few take too long to execute their mission, Mogrot's forces will be ready to march.

**24 hours after the mission begins:**
A trained rat arrives with a message from Drizzle. Mogrot has been taking meetings with his lieutenants. Patrols have doubled. Something is being planned.

**48 hours after the mission begins:**
A crude wooden platform is being assembled in the Dripfort's central pump room. Grimebacks are sharpening weapons and brewing sludge-wine. Rattooth has been seen practicing with Sizzlespear in front of a crowd, melting training dummies into puddles. Drizzle's message is terse: "Hurry."

**72 hours after the mission begins:**
The raid launches. Forty Grimebacks march east through the Blackflow toward Snotrocket territory: twenty Grimeback thugs and twenty Grimeback goblins, led by Rattooth, Skivvy, and Blort. They arrive at the Snot Pit in 4 hours. The raid inflicts catastrophic casualties on the Snotrocket Society. The adventure has failed.

## THE BLACKFLOW

The route to the Dripfort takes the Filthy Few through the Blackflow - a stretch of old industrial drainage tunnels that no gang has claimed, for good reason. The pipes here still carry intermittent surges of chemical runoff from the surface factories above.

> You leave the familiar stink of the Snot Pit behind and enter the Blackflow. The tunnel walls are stained in bands of chemical color - yellow, green, oily black - like the rings of a diseased tree. The air tastes metallic. Every few minutes, a distant rumble echoes through the pipes, and a warm, foul-smelling wind pushes through the tunnel ahead of it. Your darkvision picks up movement in the fog: shapes skittering along the walls, too big to be normal rats.

It's a two-hour trek on foot from the Snot Pit to the Dripfort. Assuming the party has a ranger, it will take 30 minutes to move through the worst section of the Blackflow at a stealthy pace, or 15 minutes moving normally. Without a ranger, it takes twice as long.

For every five minutes moving through the Blackflow stealthily, roll 1d8. On a 1, a Blackflow encounter occurs.

For every five minutes moving without stealth, roll 1d4. On a 1, a Blackflow encounter occurs.

Players may decide to attack the mutant creatures in the Blackflow proactively. If they do, the noise may attract further attention. Roll 1d4 - on a 1, an additional encounter occurs.

### Knowledge Checks

Several bits of knowledge can better prepare the Filthy Few for the Blackflow:

**DC 10 Nature/Intelligence check:** The mutant rats in the Blackflow are territorial but mostly cowardly. They swarm when they sense weakness but scatter from fire and loud, sudden noises.

**DC 14 History/Intelligence check:** The Blackflow was originally a dwarven drainage system, built centuries before the city of Blackstone existed above it. Some of the stonework down here predates everything else in the sewers. Goblins avoid the oldest sections - they say things live in the walls.

**DC 16 Arcana/Intelligence check:** The chemical runoff has a mild magical contamination - likely from an alchemist or wizard's shop above. This is what's mutating the local wildlife. Prolonged exposure is inadvisable. The worst contamination is near the factory outflow pipes, which glow faintly in the dark.

**1 - 4 Mutant Sewer Rats**

> The skittering grows louder. Four bloated rats the size of terriers emerge from a cracked pipe, their eyes glowing faintly green from chemical exposure. Their fur is patchy and their teeth are yellowed and far too long. They hiss in unison.

**2 - 1 Sewer Rat Swarm**

Pick a member of the Filthy Few, and read the following to them:

> Something brushes against your ankle. Then another something. You look down and the ground is *moving* - a carpet of small, writhing rat bodies surging toward you from a side pipe. Dozens of them. Maybe hundreds.

The sewer rat swarm gets one free attack before combat begins.

**3 - 1 Sludge Lurker**

Read this to the party's ranger or the frontmost character:

> The tunnel floor ahead is covered in a thin layer of brownish liquid. Looks like a normal puddle - the Blackflow is full of them. But something about this one feels wrong. It's too still. Too uniform in shape. And it's slightly *warm* when the toe of your boot touches the edge.

A DC 14 Perception check spots the sludge lurker before stepping in it. If undetected, the lurker attempts to engulf the first creature to step fully into its space.

**4 - 6 Mutant Sewer Rats**

> You round a bend in the pipe and find yourself face-to-face with a pack of bloated rats fighting over the remains of something that was probably a goblin once. They spot you immediately and decide you look fresher.

**5 - 1 Pipe Spider**

Read this to the party's ranger or the frontmost character:

> Your hand catches on something sticky. You pull back and a thin, translucent thread stretches from your fingers up into the darkness above. Following the thread with your darkvision, you see it connects to an elaborate web spanning the entire junction. Sitting in the center of the web, absolutely motionless, is a spider the size of a goblin.

The pipe spider attempts to ambush from above. If spotted before it attacks, the party can take a ten-minute detour around the junction to avoid it entirely. The spider does not pursue creatures that leave its web zone.

**6 - Grimeback Patrol: 4 Grimeback Thugs**

> Torchlight ahead. Voices - rough, mean-sounding goblin voices. You spot four goblins with grey clay streaks across their foreheads. Grimebacks. Armed and walking a patrol route. They haven't seen you yet.

This patrol is scouting the Blackflow perimeter. They fight to the death. If one is captured and questioned (DC 10 Intimidation), they can reveal basic information: the Dripfort has about sixty goblins, the bridge is always guarded, Mogrot rarely leaves his quarters on the upper level, and the raid is coming soon. They don't know exact details about where the magic items are kept.

## THE PUMPHOUSE

When the Filthy Few reach the edge of Grimeback territory, they emerge into a wide drainage gallery that overlooks the stronghold. Read the following:

> The Blackflow opens up and you find yourselves standing on a ledge overlooking the Dripfort. It was a pumping station once - a massive brick-and-iron structure built into a natural cavern in the sewer system. The main chamber is easily a hundred feet across and forty feet high. The old pumping machinery - huge iron wheels, pipes, and valve systems - still dominates the center of the space, though the Grimebacks have built their settlement around and on top of it.
>
> Below you, the Dripfort floor is a maze of crude shacks and lean-tos built from salvaged wood, sheet metal, and pipe sections. Catwalks made from welded grating criss-cross the upper level, connecting to platforms bolted onto the old machinery. A watchtower of stacked crates rises from the center, topped with a guttering torch.
>
> The entire structure is ringed by a moat of bubbling, greenish-brown sludge. The smell hits you from fifty feet away. One narrow pipe-bridge crosses the moat on the northern side. Even from here, you can see the goblins guarding it.
>
> To the south, the moat narrows to about fifteen feet. On the eastern side, a large outflow pipe juts from the cavern wall, sealed with a rusted iron grate.

The Dripfort is divided into two levels: the **ground floor** (the cistern floor where most of the shacks are) and the **upper level** (the catwalks and platforms attached to the old machinery, where Mogrot and his lieutenants have their quarters).

Most ground-floor structures are crude shacks with rat-hide curtain doors. Interior ceilings are about four and a half feet. The upper level is accessed by a series of iron ladders bolted to the old pump machinery.

Due to the perpetual dampness, lighting these structures on fire via natural means is nearly impossible.

## THE WAY IN

The sludge moat surrounds the Dripfort and creates a barrier for entry. The sludge is a corrosive, toxic soup of chemical waste and organic rot. Any creature that enters the sludge takes 2d6 acid damage at the start of each of its turns while in contact with it and must succeed on a DC 13 Constitution saving throw or be poisoned for 1 hour.

The sludge is about three feet deep. A creature can wade through it, but the damage and poison make this a desperate last resort.

If the characters examine the moat up close, read the following:

> The sludge moat bubbles and churns with a slow, sick rhythm. The surface is an oily greenish-brown that reflects nothing. As you watch, a rat scurries down the bank and pauses at the edge to drink. Its nose touches the surface. It squeals, staggers sideways, and collapses twitching into the sludge. A moment later, only bubbles remain where it was.

Non-organic objects like wood, metal, and stone are unaffected by the sludge. A wooden plank laid across a narrow section could work as a bridge, provided the span is short enough.

### The Pipe Bridge (Guarded Entrance)

The pipe bridge is a twenty-foot section of large-diameter iron pipe laid across the moat on the northern side. It is well-guarded.

**Six Grimeback thugs** are stationed at the bridge in pairs, one pair at each end and one pair in the middle. Each station has a large torch providing bright light to 30 feet and dim light for an additional 20 feet. The entire bridge is lit. The pipe rings hollowly underfoot.

Goblins attempting Stealth across the bridge suffer a -8 penalty on any checks to do so.

It is possible to crawl under the bridge along the outer rim of the moat. A DC 8 Athletics/Strength check is required to traverse the underside without slipping into the sludge. A Stealth check is then made against the guards' passive Perception of 10.

### Gunk's Throw

If the Filthy Few made an alliance with Gunk and he traveled ahead of the crew, he will be waiting at the south end of the sludge moat. There, he sits on a chunk of fallen masonry, picking his nose with a finger the size of a goblin's forearm.

> As you approach Gunk, he looks up from his nose-mining and grins. He stands - his head nearly touching the cavern ceiling - and cracks his knuckles. He peers across the fifteen feet of bubbling sludge and then back down at you.
>
> "Alllright. Who go first?"

Gunk is poised to throw characters approximately 30 feet - more than enough to clear the moat. After deciding which goblin will be thrown first, Gunk makes a Strength check (+5) to throw the goblin. See the Gunk Throw Chart for the result.

### Gunk Throw Chart (Make a Strength check (+5) for Gunk.)

| Roll | Result |
|------|--------|
| 1–6 | Gunk throws the goblin quite short and they land in the middle of the sludge moat, about 8 feet from the far side (and 7 feet back to Gunk). See the sludge moat rules above. |
| 7–10 | Gunk throws the goblin but they hit the low wall bordering the Dripfort floor. The goblin takes 1d4 bludgeoning damage and the impact is loud. Roll 1d6 - on a 1, the clamor draws 1d4 **Grimeback goblins** from a nearby shack. |
| 11–19 | Gunk throws the goblin clear over the moat. They land on the far side and must make a DC 12 Dexterity saving throw or take 1d6 bludgeoning damage from the hard landing. |
| 20+ | Gunk lofts the goblin in a smooth, precise arc. They land softly on a pile of garbage on the far side. No damage, no noise. |

Once one character makes it across, they can drag garbage near the landing site to cushion falls. Subsequent successful throws (11+) no longer require a saving throw.

A rope can be tied between a pipe on Gunk's side and a fixture on the Dripfort wall. Shimmying across requires a DC 10 Acrobatics check to avoid falling into the sludge.

After everyone is across, Gunk scoops up any remaining rat payment and lumbers back toward his bridge. If the Filthy Few request he wait, Gunk can be persuaded with a DC 14 Persuasion check.

### Fizzpop's Scumskimmer

If the Filthy Few recruited Fizzpop, she has set up the Scumskimmer on the eastern edge of the moat.

> Fizzpop stands beside her contraption, beaming with pride. The Scumskimmer is... technically a boat. It's a barrel cut in half, coated in a waxy grey substance that smells like troll, with two crude paddles lashed to the sides and a small bellows on the back that serves as either a rudder or a weapon. It's unclear.
>
> She pats it lovingly.
>
> "Fits three per trip. Paddle slow, paddle quiet. Paddle fast, paddle loud, paddle probably tip over and die. Questions? No? Good, good, good."

The Scumskimmer carries up to three Small creatures per trip. Paddling slowly and quietly takes 2 minutes per crossing and requires a DC 8 Dexterity check from the paddler. On a failure, the Scumskimmer rocks dangerously - each passenger must succeed on a DC 10 Dexterity saving throw or fall into the sludge.

Paddling quickly takes 30 seconds per crossing but requires a DC 14 Dexterity check and the splashing attracts attention. Roll 1d6 - on a 1 or 2, a Grimeback patrol on the inner bank spots the boat.

### The Grate

The sealed outflow pipe on the eastern wall is covered by a rusted iron grate. It can be examined from the moat's edge.

> A large pipe - big enough for two goblins side by side - juts from the cavern wall above the sludge line. It's sealed with an iron grate that has seen better centuries. Rust has eaten through several of the bars, and a thin trickle of foul water drips from the pipe's interior.

The weakened bars can be broken with a DC 12 Strength check or cut with tools (5 minutes of work). The pipe leads directly into the back of **Location 8: The Larder**.

The noise from breaking the grate requires a DC 12 Stealth check to muffle. On a failure, 1d4 **Grimeback goblins** investigate from the Larder in 2 rounds.

A grappling hook can be used to create a secure rope connection from the cavern wall to the grate, allowing characters to cross above the sludge. Successfully throwing the hook requires a DC 14 Dexterity check. If the check fails by 5 or more, the hook falls into the sludge and the rope is consumed.

## ALERTING THE PUMPHOUSE

There are several points in the mission where the Grimebacks may become aware of intruders. Fights should be avoided at all costs, but occasionally the Filthy Few will make a mistake and risk alerting the settlement.

In the case that a loud fight breaks out, sleeping goblins within 40 feet will be alerted. Sleeping goblins must succeed on a DC 10 Perception check with disadvantage to wake. Assume 1d4 goblins sleep within 40 feet at most points in the Dripfort.

Goblins waking from sleep will spend one round shaking off grogginess before approaching any disturbance. Goblins who spot intruders react appropriately: either entering the fight or running to alert more combat-ready goblins.

If a goblin escapes, they will run to the barracks or the pipe bridge. They return in 3 rounds with 1d6 **Grimeback thugs**. Be sure to subtract the numbers from those locations.

If the watchtower alarm is raised (a tin-can-and-chain contraption that sends a clattering racket throughout the Dripfort), the entire settlement wakes within 2 rounds. At that point, the mission becomes a desperate fighting retreat or a very creative escape.

### Resting

Resting on the outer edge of the Dripfort is possible, provided the Grimebacks have not been alerted to the Filthy Few's presence. But there's always a danger of encountering a patrol. For every hour resting, roll 1d12. On a 1, a Grimeback patrol of 4 **Grimeback thugs** and 1 **Grimeback hexer** discovers the crew and attacks.

## DAYTIME OBSERVATION

Attempting to enter the Dripfort during waking hours is extremely dangerous and counterproductive to the mission. However, if the Filthy Few decide to observe the Dripfort during the day, you may read the following:

> As the distant rumble of surface-world morning echoes through the pipes above, the Dripfort comes alive. Grimebacks emerge from their shacks, stretching and scratching. Some head for the kitchens, others take up guard positions. A group of goblins heads out through the pipe bridge on a patrol shift. On the upper catwalks, you see larger goblins moving between the platform rooms - lieutenants, by the look of them.

The following is automatically observed as the crew spies on the settlement:

- Mogrot's quarters are on the upper level, identifiable by the number of goblins coming and going throughout the day. Two guards are always posted outside his door.
- The fighting pit sees regular use during the day. Several brawls break out, drawing crowds of spectators.
- Armed patrols leave through the pipe bridge every few hours and return later.
- Goblins bring food from the larder to the kitchens throughout the morning.

Additionally, have the characters make an Investigation/Intelligence check. Based on the highest result, the following is also observed:

**DC 10:** You spot a familiar goblin face working in the kitchens. Shorter than the others, keeping her head down. That's Drizzle - the Snotrocket spy.

**DC 15:** In the fighting pit, you see a stocky goblin warming up with a short greenish spear. Each practice thrust leaves a hissing, smoking mark on whatever it hits. Other goblins give him a wide berth. That's Rattooth, with Sizzlespear.

**DC 20:** On the upper catwalk, you spot a goblin whose armor appears to be... *moving*. Squirming. The goblin flinches and twitches as he walks, occasionally slapping at himself. You can hear faint squeaking from across the cavern. That's Skivvy, wearing the Rat King's Hide.

Note that actively spying on the Dripfort from the outer ledge is well within the Filthy Few's expertise, so no check is required to go undetected during observation.

## PUMPHOUSE LOCATIONS

### General Shacks

The unnumbered buildings on the map house ordinary Grimeback goblins. After 10 PM, each shack holds 1d4–1 sleeping **Grimeback goblins**. They sleep on the ground atop piles of rags and rat-hide blankets.

Searching through a room inhabited by sleeping goblins requires a successful DC 10 Stealth check. Failing this check means you've made enough noise to rouse one of the goblins. The sleeping goblin wakes, sees the intruders, and combat immediately starts.

Each successful Stealth check results in finding one goblin trinket (see Appendix T: Goblin Trinkets).

In addition to trinkets, the following items are readily available in each shack:

- 1d6 clay cups
- A bucket of grey water
- A rat-hide blanket
- 1d4 chewed rat bones

### 1. THE GAMBLING DEN

A large shack with three of its four walls removed, covered by a patchwork tarp roof. As the crew approaches, read the following:

> The unmistakable sounds of goblin gambling reach your ears - dice clattering on wood, goblins cursing, someone demanding a recount, and the occasional wet *thwack* of someone being hit for suspected cheating.

If the Filthy Few investigate before 2AM, read the following:

> Under the tarp, five goblins hunch around a table playing dice bones, snarling at each other over every roll. A pile of copper bits and small trinkets sits in the middle of the table.
>
> At a second table, three goblins are playing Rat Race - a game involving live rats, a wooden track, and a great deal of shouting. The rats do not appear to be cooperating. One has escaped and is being chased under the table by a furious goblin.
>
> Sitting apart from the others on a stool in the shadows, a goblin in a heavy, filthy cloak watches everything. He's unnervingly still. One hand rests inside the cloak. The other holds a cup he hasn't drunk from.

The cloaked goblin is **Blort**, the Grimeback shaman, who possesses the **Cloak of Filth**. He keeps the cloak on at all times - it's his most prized possession. His familiar, a **sewer imp** named Snik, lurks invisibly nearby, occasionally nudging dice at Blort's silent direction.

Blort is using Snik to subtly influence the dice games - keeping himself from losing too badly while keeping the other goblins entertained and distracted. An observer would need to succeed on a DC 20 Perception check to notice the imp's invisible hand nudging a die mid-roll.

Gambling continues until 2AM. The den is populated with:

- 5 **Grimeback goblins** playing dice bones
- 3 **Grimeback goblins** playing Rat Race
- **Blort** (wearing the Cloak of Filth)
- Snik (invisible **sewer imp**)
- 2 off-duty **Grimeback thugs** drinking in the corner

When gambling ends at 2AM (Blort will still have his cloak), Blort proceeds to his workshop (**Location 5**) and the rest of the goblins either stumble to their shacks or pass out where they sit.

If the Filthy Few investigate the den after 2AM, read the following:

> Three goblins lie face-down on the dice table, snoring into puddles of spilled sludge-wine. One is clutching a rat from the Rat Race track - the rat is also asleep. At the second table, a single naked goblin lies on his back, grumbling and scratching himself. He appears to have lost everything, including his dignity.

The three goblins on the dice table are **Grimeback goblins**. The naked goblin is an off-duty **Grimeback thug** (no armor, no weapons).

If the goblins here are looted before they leave at 2AM, the following treasure is found scattered among their possessions:

- 187 copper pieces
- A potion of healing (heals 2d8 hit points)
- A potion of sickness (looks and smells identical to the healing potion - deals 2d8 poison damage if drunk)
- A cracked magnifying glass (worth 2 gp)
- 6 tarnished silver rings (worth 2 sp each)
- A charcoal sketch of a human woman, rolled up in a tube (worth 5 gp in a human settlement; worthless to goblins)
- A tiger eye gemstone (worth 10 gp)

The two potions are both being used as betting stakes at the dice table. They look and smell identical.

### 2. THE WATCHTOWER

> A ramshackle tower stands near the center of the Dripfort, built from stacked crates, scrap wood, and an alarming amount of optimism. It rises about twenty feet - the highest point on the ground floor. A goblin-sized platform sits at the top, surrounded by a railing of bent pipe. A tin-can alarm system dangles from the railing. You can see the chain that triggers it running down the tower's side.

Two **Grimeback thugs** man the watchtower at all times, rotating every 4 hours. From the platform, they can see the entire ground floor, the pipe bridge, and most of the moat.

The watchtower has a blind spot: the area directly beneath it and the narrow alley between Locations 5 and 6 are in permanent shadow and difficult to observe from the platform above.

Climbing the tower from the outside requires a DC 12 Athletics check. The structure sways with each movement - the climber must also succeed on a DC 10 Stealth check or the guards feel the movement and look down.

If the alarm chain is pulled, the entire Dripfort is alerted within 2 rounds.

The alarm chain can be cut or disconnected. From the ground, a character can reach the lowest section of chain with a DC 14 Dexterity check (using a blade on a pole, a thrown dagger, etc.). From the tower platform, simply unhooking the chain requires no check but does require getting past the guards.

### 3. THE BARRACKS

> A long, low, rectangular structure built from sheet metal and wooden planks. The sound of industrial-grade goblin snoring reverberates from within. The rat-hide curtain over the entrance flutters with each collective exhale.

These are the primary barracks for the Dripfort. Twelve **Grimeback thugs** sleep here in shifts - at any given time, 8 are sleeping and 4 are on duty elsewhere. They sleep in piles on the floor with their weapons beside them. There are several open gaps in the walls that serve as windows.

Sneaking into the barracks is a risky maneuver, requiring a DC 10 Stealth check to avoid waking anyone. There isn't much to uncover near the sleeping goblins, but rummaging through sacks (each requiring an additional DC 10 Stealth check) yields one goblin trinket per successful search (up to 8).

There are two wooden chests in this room. They are unlocked and stocked with:

- 14 scimitars
- 6 shortbows with quivers
- 8 sets of leather armor
- A locked iron box (DC 12 to pick with thieves' tools) containing 45 silver pieces, a set of thieves' tools, and a tarnished bronze whistle that makes no audible sound when blown (it summons rats - 1d4 **mutant sewer rats** arrive in 1d4 rounds)

### 4. THE FIGHTING PIT

> A circular depression has been scooped out of the Dripfort floor, about fifteen feet across and three feet deep. The walls of the pit are stained dark with old blood and gouged with claw marks. Broken teeth and scraps of leather litter the bottom. Crude bleachers made from stacked pipes and crates ring the pit.

The fighting pit is where the Grimebacks settle disputes, provide entertainment, and occasionally execute prisoners. During waking hours, it sees regular use. At night, the pit is empty.

The pit makes a convenient hiding spot if the Filthy Few need to duck out of sight - it's below the sightline of casual passers-by and the watchtower's view.

Searching the pit floor yields: 2 goblin teeth (worthless), a dented brass knuckle-duster (functions as a club), and a **sewer poultice** (heals 2d6 HP after 60 minutes) that someone dropped and forgot about.

### 5. BLORT'S WORKSHOP

> *Blort speaks in a low, scratchy whisper. He pauses mid-sentence to listen to things that aren't there.*

> This shack is set apart from the others, wedged into a corner where two old pipes meet the cavern wall. Unlike the other structures, this one has an actual door - a heavy wooden slab with three different locks on it. Strange smells waft from inside: burnt hair, sulfur, and something sweet and wrong.

The door is locked with three separate locks. Each requires a DC 12 Dexterity check with thieves' tools to pick, or the door can be forced open with a DC 18 Strength check (which is extremely loud and automatically alerts goblins within 60 feet).

If the Filthy Few get inside, read the following:

> The interior of this shack looks like a wizard's study as interpreted by a goblin with no formal education and a dangerous amount of curiosity. Shelves line every wall, crammed with jars of unidentifiable substances, bundles of dried rats, stacks of crudely bound books, and a human skull that has been repurposed as a candle holder. A workbench dominates the center, covered in arcane scribblings, crushed herbs, and a mortar and pestle caked with something green.
>
> In the back corner, a reed mattress serves as Blort's bed. A wooden rack on the wall holds three wands of varying size and condition.

Blort arrives here after gambling ends at 2AM. He works on his experiments until roughly 5AM, then sleeps. If the crew arrives while Blort is present, he is wearing the Cloak of Filth and his familiar Snik is invisibly present.

If Blort is aware of intruders, he immediately activates the Cloak's invisibility (smearing filth from a bucket he keeps by the door over himself) and attempts to flee to alert Mogrot on the upper level. He does *not* attack while invisible - he knows about the paralysis curse.

If the Filthy Few make noise getting into the workshop while Blort is present, Snik (who has exceptional hearing) wakes and secretly wakes Blort. Blort goes invisible and attempts to slip past the intruders to escape.

A member of the Filthy Few can spot Blort's invisible form slipping past with a successful DC 16 Perception check.

**The wand rack contains:**

- A wand of magic detection (3 charges, regains 1d3 at dawn)
- A wand of grease (5 charges, regains 1d4+1 at dawn, DC 12 Dex save or fall prone in a 10-foot square)
- A wand of stink (7 charges, produces a 20-foot cloud of nauseating gas centered on a point within 30 feet; creatures in the area must succeed on a DC 12 Con save or spend their action retching; cloud lasts 1 round)

**Blort's workbench contains:**

- 3 vials of acid (as acid vial)
- 2 sewer poultices
- A scroll of Detect Magic
- A journal written in Goblin detailing Blort's experiments. A DC 10 Intelligence check to read reveals: Blort has been studying the three stolen magic items and has concluded that all three are dangerous to their users. He's been trying to warn Mogrot, but Mogrot won't listen. The journal also contains a crude but accurate map of the upper level, including the location of Mogrot's quarters.

**Treasure:**

- 67 silver pieces in a clay pot under the bed
- A jade figurine of a rat (worth 25 gp)
- 2 goblin trinkets

### 6. THE KITCHENS

> The smell hits you before you see the building - a complex bouquet of boiled rat, burned cabbage, and something that might once have been cheese. A wide shack with a smoke-hole cut in the roof serves as the Dripfort's communal kitchen. Pots and pans hang from hooks on the walls. A large cauldron bubbles over a low fire in the center.

This is where the Grimebacks' meals are prepared. The kitchen is staffed by **Curdface**, a **Grimeback goblin** who takes his cooking extremely seriously (and personally), and 2 other **Grimeback goblins** who serve as his assistants.

The kitchen operates from early morning until late evening. After midnight, it's empty except for the simmering cauldron (which contains a perpetual stew that is never allowed to stop cooking - new ingredients are simply added on top of whatever came before).

**Drizzle**, the Snotrocket spy, works here as a kitchen assistant. If the crew arrives during working hours, she can be spotted with a DC 10 Investigation check - she's the one keeping her head down and not making eye contact with anyone.

If the Filthy Few can get a private moment with Drizzle (requiring either waiting until after midnight when the kitchen is empty, or creating a distraction to draw Curdface and the other assistants away), she can provide crucial intelligence. See **Speaking with Drizzle** below.

The kitchen contains:

- A butcher's cleaver (functions as a handaxe)
- Several large wooden spoons (function as clubs)
- A full set of cooking implements
- Enough Toadphlegm Soup ingredients to feed sixty goblins
- A cask of sludge-wine (mildly alcoholic, tastes like swamp water filtered through a sock; 1 cup grants advantage on the next Charisma check made within 1 hour but disadvantage on the next Dexterity check)

### SPEAKING WITH DRIZZLE

> *Drizzle speaks in a rapid, hushed voice. Her eyes dart constantly toward the door.*

If the Filthy Few manage to speak privately with Drizzle, she provides the following information freely:

**Q: Where is Sizzlespear?**
Rattooth carries it everywhere. He sleeps with it. During the day, he's usually in the fighting pit or on patrol. At night, he's in his shack near the goat pen. (**Location 9.**)

**Q: Where is the Rat King's Hide?**
Skivvy wears it constantly. He says he can't take it off - and Drizzle isn't sure he's lying about that. At night, he's usually in Mogrot's quarters on the upper level. He's been spending a lot of time up there lately. (**Location 11.**)

**Q: Where is the Cloak of Filth?**
Blort has it. He wears it everywhere. He uses the invisibility to spy and snoop, never to fight. He's paranoid and smart - the hardest of the three to catch off guard. His workshop is locked with three locks. (**Location 5.**)

**Q: Where is Mogrot?**
Upper level. He almost never comes down. Two guards outside his door at all times, more inside. His quarters are the old pump control room - thick walls, one door, no windows. Very hard to get in quietly.

**Q: What's in the copper pot?**
Drizzle doesn't know. She's never been allowed on the upper level. But she's overheard Blort muttering about it - something about "not feeding it" and "keeping the lid sealed." Whatever it is, Blort is afraid of it.

**Q: How do we escape?**
Drizzle has been breeding and training giant sewer roaches in a hidden pen behind the larder (**Location 8**). They're big enough to ride and fast enough to outrun goblins on foot. They can skitter across the sludge moat - their legs are long enough to keep their bodies above the surface. Drizzle has four roaches saddled and ready.

**Q: Why didn't you report on Skivvy being in Mogrot's quarters?**
Drizzle looks uncomfortable. She didn't know until recently. Skivvy and Mogrot's daughter, **Gristla**, have been secretly meeting. Skivvy sneaks up to the upper level after midnight. If the crew goes after the Rat King's Hide at night, Skivvy won't be in his own shack - he'll be in Gristla's room on the upper level.

Beyond these specifics, Drizzle has excellent knowledge of the Dripfort and can answer most questions about the layout, guard schedules, and daily routines.

### 7. THE GOAT PEN

> You approach a low stone wall, maybe three feet high, enclosing a muddy area about thirty feet across. The smell of animal dung mixes with the ever-present sewer stink. Several sets of small, alien eyes peer at you over the wall. After a moment, you realize you're looking at goats.

The Grimebacks keep a small herd of 12 goats, stolen in surface raids over the past year. The goats provide milk, meat, and bones. They are surprisingly content living underground and have grown fat on a diet of garbage and sewer mushrooms.

A 15-foot by 15-foot shack is attached to the pen's northern side, serving as both a shelter for the goats and quarters for Rattooth. The shack has two rooms: the indoor pen (**7a**) and Rattooth's quarters (**7b**).

If a goat is attacked, the rest panic - jumping the wall and scattering throughout the Dripfort. This creates significant noise and chaos that could serve as an excellent distraction... or a disaster.

**7a. Indoor Pen**

> Three particularly fat goats lie on a pile of straw in the corner. Shelves along the wall hold crude animal-husbandry tools: feeders, buckets, and something that looks disturbingly like a goat-milking machine cobbled together from pipe fittings. A wooden gate on the north wall leads to a second room. A heavy door on the east wall is secured with an iron bar from this side.

The heavy door leads to the outdoor pen. The wooden gate leads to Rattooth's quarters (**7b**) and is unlocked.

**7b. Rattooth's Quarters**

This is where Rattooth sleeps. If the crew arrives before 2AM, the room is empty - Rattooth is either in the fighting pit, on patrol, or drinking. After 2AM, he's here, sleeping.

> A bare room. A mattress of piled rags on the floor. A stack of goat bones in the corner - trophies or chew toys, it's hard to say. A battered journal lies open on the floor next to the mattress. And there, leaning against the wall beside the bed, within arm's reach of the sleeping goblin - a short spear with a greenish, glistening tip that hisses faintly, as though the air itself is offended by its presence.

The sleeping goblin is **Rattooth**. **Sizzlespear** is one of the three magic items the crew has been sent to steal.

Sneaking toward Rattooth and the spear requires a DC 12 Stealth check. If Rattooth is woken, he grabs Sizzlespear immediately - it's within arm's reach.

**Taking Sizzlespear:** If a character attempts to carefully lift the spear while Rattooth sleeps, they must succeed on a DC 14 Sleight of Hand check. On a failure, the spear's tip brushes the wall, leaving a hissing acid burn that wakes Rattooth instantly.

**Assassinating Rattooth:** If one creature attacks Rattooth while he is sleeping, award them an automatic critical hit. Begin combat as normal on the following turn. If the entire crew moves in to assassinate him simultaneously, he has no chance to fight back and can be assumed killed automatically.

**Fighting Rattooth:** Rattooth is a well-trained fighter and does not suffer the penalty of a normal surprise round when awakened. He knows he is too far from the barracks to get help quickly, so he fights to the death with the goal of making as much noise as possible - including using Sizzlespear's acid AoE to maximum effect, regardless of damage to himself.

He sleeps with a grappling hook under his pillow and can pick it up on his first turn as a free action.

**Rattooth's journal** is written in Goblin and details his training routines, patrol observations, and kill counts from recent skirmishes. Towards the back, the entries become shorter and more pained. The acid from Sizzlespear has been burning his hands during every fight. He writes:

> *"Used the spear on the Snotrocket scouts yesterday. Killed two. The spray got me too. Always gets me. Hands are blistering again. Hurts to hold things. Mogrot says stop complaining. Easy for him to say, fat bastard just sits up there."*

**Treasure:**

- Sizzlespear (see Appendix A)
- A grappling hook
- 23 copper pieces in a leather pouch
- 1 goblin trinket
- A crude charcoal map of the Snotrocket tunnels (Rattooth's scouting intelligence - this would be valuable to bring back to Scab)

### 8. THE LARDER

> A squat, solid-looking structure near the eastern wall of the Dripfort. Unlike the other shacks, this one has a proper door - wooden, with a heavy latch. The smell coming from inside is a complex layering of cured meat, mold, and brine.

The larder stores the Grimebacks' food supply: cured rats, barrels of pickled mushrooms, sacks of stolen grain, jars of rendered fat, and a truly impressive collection of things that may or may not be edible.

The door is latched but not locked. Inside:

> Shelves and barrels line every wall. Cured rat carcasses hang from hooks on the ceiling. The floor is sticky. In the back corner, a section of the wall has been crudely knocked out, revealing the large outflow pipe that leads to the outer cavern wall and the rusted grate.

If the crew entered through the grate, this is where they emerge into the Dripfort.

Behind a stack of barrels in the larder's far corner is a hidden pen where **Drizzle** has been secretly raising **4 giant sewer roaches** - her planned escape route. The roaches are large enough to ride (treat as mounts), skittish but trained to follow simple commands from Drizzle. Their long legs allow them to cross the sludge moat without submerging their bodies.

Getting a roach saddled and ready requires a DC 12 Animal Handling check (Drizzle can assist, granting advantage). If magic is used to speak with the roaches, they cooperate readily - they hate this place and want to leave.

**Treasure:**

- Enough food to supply 10 days of rations for the entire crew
- 2 jars of rendered troll fat (each jar coats one Small creature, granting resistance to acid damage for 1 hour - extremely useful if anyone falls in the sludge moat)
- 1 goblin trinket
- A cask of surprisingly decent surface-world ale (worth 5 gp to anyone who drinks ale; priceless to a goblin)

### 9. THE BARBERY

> *Skivvy speaks in clipped, military-sounding sentences. He scratches himself constantly.*

> As you approach this shack, you hear goblin music - a grinding, atonal melody played on instruments that sound like they were built from garbage. Because they were. Dim reddish light leaks from gaps in the walls.

If the crew arrives before 2AM, read the following:

> Inside, chaos. A band of three goblins plays tin-pipe instruments while a fourth pounds on an overturned bucket. A dozen goblins are spread around the room drinking sludge-wine from clay cups. In the center of the room, a large stool serves as a barber's chair. A goblin holding an oversized pair of rusty shears dances around it with moves as erratic as the beat. Another goblin sits rigidly in the chair, watching the shears with visible terror.
>
> With a *snip*, the barber lops off a chunk of hair. The crowd cheers.

This is the Dripfort's social hub. Drink night at the barbery is the highlight of the week for the common Grimebacks.

The barbery is populated with:

- 8 **Grimeback goblins** drinking and watching
- 4 off-duty **Grimeback thugs** (no armor, weapons nearby)
- **Mooney** the barber, a **Grimeback goblin** who uses his shears as a dagger if threatened
- The band (3 **Grimeback goblins** with improvised instruments)

If the crew sticks around watching through the walls for 20 minutes, a brawl breaks out when Mooney accidentally clips a thug's ear. The brawl provides an excellent distraction for thieving activities elsewhere in the Dripfort - for about 10 minutes, every goblin nearby is either fighting, watching, or betting on the outcome.

Mooney holds the evening's take (95 copper pieces) in a belt pouch. There's also half a gallon of sludge-wine left in a clay jug near the band.

If the crew approaches after 2AM, the barbery is empty except for two passed-out goblins and a lot of spilled sludge-wine.

---

### 10. THE REFUSE HEAP

> At the back of the Dripfort, piled against the cavern wall, is a mountain of garbage. Thirty feet high and sprawling. The collective waste of sixty goblins over months of habitation. The smell is... indescribable. Even by sewer goblin standards, it's impressive.

The refuse heap is exactly what it looks like: a massive pile of trash. However, it serves several functions for the adventurous crew:

- **Hiding:** The heap provides total cover and concealment. No goblin willingly goes near it unless they're dumping more trash. A creature hiding in the heap has advantage on Stealth checks but must succeed on a DC 10 Constitution save every 10 minutes or become poisoned for 1 hour from the fumes.

- **Climbing:** The heap is piled against the cavern wall, reaching nearly to the level of the upper catwalks. A DC 10 Athletics check allows a character to climb the heap. A DC 12 check at the top allows them to reach the catwalk railing and pull themselves onto the upper level - bypassing the guarded ladders entirely.

- **Treasure:** A DC 14 Investigation check while digging through the heap (a 10-minute activity requiring a Constitution save as above) uncovers: 34 copper pieces, a perfectly good dagger that someone threw away by accident, a pair of goblin-sized boots in decent condition, and a small locked box (DC 10 to pick) containing a **potion of climbing** (grants a climbing speed equal to walking speed for 1 hour).

---

### 11. THE UPPER LEVEL - MOGROT'S DOMAIN

The upper level of the Dripfort is constructed on the old pump machinery's catwalks and platforms. It's accessed from the ground floor by two iron ladders on opposite sides of the central machinery, or by climbing the refuse heap to reach the catwalk.

The catwalks are iron grating - movement across them is noisy unless a creature succeeds on a DC 12 Stealth check with each movement. The catwalks connect three rooms built on the old machinery platforms.

**11a. Guard Platform**

The first platform at the top of either ladder serves as a guard post. Two **Grimeback thugs** stand watch here at all times. They are alert and have a clear view of both ladders and the catwalks.

The guards rotate at 2AM. During the rotation, there is a two-minute window where the platform is unguarded as the new shift climbs up and the old shift climbs down.

**11b. Gristla's Room**

> This small room is surprisingly well-appointed by goblin standards. A mattress with an actual blanket. A cracked mirror mounted on the wall. Shelves with small trinkets and a collection of colorful surface-world bottles that have been arranged decoratively.

**Gristla** is Mogrot's daughter, a **Grimeback hexer** in her own right. She lives in this room and spends most of her time here. After midnight, **Skivvy** sneaks up to spend time with her. Their relationship is secret - if discovered, Mogrot would be furious.

If the crew enters the room after midnight, read the following:

> A goblin in squirming, squeaking rat-hide armor sits on a wobbly stool, reading aloud from a crumpled piece of paper. His voice trembles with what might be passion or might be pain from the armor biting him.
>
> "Your eyes are like... two puddles. Good puddles. Not sludge puddles. Clean ones. With a... a nice smell."
>
> On the bed, a female goblin lies on her side, chin propped on her fist, listening with an expression that might be adoration or might be pity. It's hard to tell with goblins.

Skivvy, wearing the **Rat King's Hide**, is reading terrible poetry to Gristla.

If the crew attacks the pair, they fight for two rounds before attempting to flee and get help from the guards on the platform. If any noise is made, the two guards on the platform (**11a**) arrive at the end of the first round of combat.

If Skivvy is killed, the Rat King's Hide activates its death-swarm curse - the rats unravel from his corpse and surge toward the killer. DC 18 Strength check or become the armor's new wearer.

**Treasure scattered around the room:**

- 234 silver pieces in a small wooden box
- A painted porcelain figurine of an elven woman (worth 30 gp)
- A small black pearl necklace (worth 100 gp - Skivvy's gift to Gristla, not yet given)
- A brass hand mirror (worth 5 gp)
- An ornamental bronze helmet too small for anyone to actually wear (worth 10 gp)

**11c. Mogrot's Quarters - The Control Room**

This is the old pump control room - a reinforced chamber with thick iron walls and one heavy door. Two **Grimeback thugs** stand guard outside the door at all times. The door is locked from the inside; it can be picked with a DC 15 Dexterity check with thieves' tools, or forced open with a DC 18 Strength check (which is deafeningly loud).

The control room has a single small window - a ventilation slit in the iron wall, barely six inches wide. A goblin could not fit through it, but a familiar, a very small creature, or a well-aimed object could.

If the crew gets inside, read the following:

> The control room is dominated by the original pump controls - huge iron wheels, valve handles, and pressure gauges, all long since rusted into immobility. The Grimebacks have built around them, draping the machinery in stolen tapestries and piling stolen goods into every corner.
>
> In the center of the room, on a throne constructed from a cracked toilet bolted to a wooden platform, sits the fattest goblin you have ever seen. He is easily three times the width of a normal goblin. His body overflows the toilet-throne on all sides. He wears a crown made from bent copper pipe and a necklace of rat skulls. He is snoring. Loudly.
>
> To the right of the throne, a plain copper pot - about two feet tall and a foot wide - sits on an iron stand. Its lid is sealed shut with wax. Something about it makes your skin crawl. The air near it feels wrong.

This is **Mogrot the Wide**, leader of the Grimebacks. He sleeps in his control room every night, and rarely leaves. He is a level 13 Warlock (see Bestiary) - awakening him is catastrophic.

Not using Stealth in this room automatically wakes him. Touching the copper pot also automatically wakes him.

Any time an action is made to move across the room, the character must succeed on a DC 10 Stealth check to avoid waking Mogrot.

**The Copper Pot:** Scab warned the crew not to touch it. He was right. The pot contains a bound **sludge elemental** - a creature Mogrot bargained with and trapped. If the wax seal is broken and the lid opened, the elemental erupts in a 20-foot radius explosion of corrosive sludge. Every creature in the area takes 8d6 acid damage (DC 14 Dex save for half). The elemental then goes on a rampage, attacking everything in the Dripfort indiscriminately for 1d4 minutes before dissipating.

Opening the pot is almost certainly suicidal - but it could also serve as a desperate, apocalyptic distraction if the crew is cornered and out of options.

If the pot is disturbed, or Mogrot awakens, read the following:

> The fat goblin's eyes snap open. They're an unnatural orange, like embers. He doesn't look surprised. He looks *annoyed*.
>
> "Who dares?"
>
> He doesn't wait for an answer. The air around him shimmers with heat, and green fire licks at the tips of his sausage-like fingers. The rusted valve wheels on the walls begin to glow.

**Fighting Mogrot:** Mogrot is a level 13 Warlock and massively outclasses the Filthy Few. He fights with eldritch blasts, summoned sludge, and sheer magical force. He is overconfident and believes he can destroy the intruders easily (he's probably right). He fights to the death.

If the crew retreats, Mogrot does not pursue - he's too fat and too lazy. But he will raise the alarm, waddle to the control room door, and bellow for his guards. The entire Dripfort is alerted within 2 rounds.

Mogrot has a **final eruption** ability: when he drops to 0 HP, his body explodes in a burst of green flame. Every creature within 15 feet takes 6d6 fire damage (DC 14 Dex save for half).

**The chests in the room are not trapped but are locked (DC 12 to pick). They contain:**

- Chest One: 3 potions of healing (heals 4d4+4 hit points each)
- Chest Two: A dagger +1, a vial of Oil of Sharpness (coat a weapon for +3 to attack and damage for 1 hour), and a Bag of Sewer Beans (see Appendix A)
- Chest Three: 432 gold pieces, 83 platinum pieces
- Chest Four: 3 fire opals (worth 500 gp each)

## ESCAPING THE PUMPHOUSE

Drizzle's plan to escape the Dripfort is via the **giant sewer roaches** hidden in the larder (**Location 8**). However, the players may come up with their own methods. Whether they use the goats as a diversion, keep Gunk around to pull them back across the moat, ride the Scumskimmer, or simply run for it - be sure to encourage creative escape routes.

The most dangerous route is evacuating across the pipe bridge. If attempted without stealth, this attracts the attention of all bridge guards, culminating in a final battle.

Running from the Dripfort with Grimebacks in pursuit is a challenge. Retreating through the Blackflow will attract random encounters - roll on the Blackflow encounter table every 5 minutes (1 on a d4 triggers an encounter). The Grimebacks will pursue through the Blackflow for 15 minutes before returning to the Dripfort to regroup.

The giant sewer roaches are fast enough to outrun goblin pursuit. Once mounted and across the sludge moat, the crew can reach the Snot Pit in approximately 30 minutes at full roach speed.

---

## ENDING THE ADVENTURE

Once the Filthy Few have escaped the Dripfort with the magic items, the adventure is over. Upon return, the Snotrocket navy escorts them across the dark water to the trash island in a procession of washtubs and barrel rafts, goblins banging oars against the hulls in celebration. Surviving members of the crew are greeted as heroes within the Snot Pit and granted the title of **Gloop Legend** by King Scab.

The crew has recovered the three stolen magic items. Whether Scab decides to use them, destroy them, or lock them away is a story for another day - but the threat of the Grimeback raid has been neutralized.

If you'd like to continue the story, several hooks are available:

- The Grimebacks, humiliated by the theft, may seek revenge. Mogrot (if he survived) is not the forgiving type.
- The magic items' drawbacks may cause problems for whoever ends up using them. The Rat King's Hide in particular has a tendency to find new owners whether they want it or not.
- Scab's ambitions are growing. The Snot Pit is secure, but a king needs more than one victory. He may send the Filthy Few on increasingly dangerous errands - expanding Snotrocket territory, forging alliances with other underground factions, or investigating rumors of powerful artifacts in the deep tunnels.
- The copper pot, if left intact, is still sitting in Mogrot's quarters. Whatever was in it is still in there. For now.
- Something in the deep Blackflow has noticed all the activity. The old dwarven tunnels go deeper than any goblin has explored. Things live down there. Old things.

The Filthy Few and this adventure could mark the beginning of a weird and wonderful campaign.

### King Scab
*(Quest Giver - does not accompany the crew)*

The recently crowned king of the Snotrocket Society. Small even by goblin standards, with darting eyes and a perpetually twitching nose. Wears the old leather boot of King Battle Battle Wee as his crown and carries the rusty meat cleaver that opened the former king's throat. Once the weakest goblin in the sewers, Scab rose to power through an improbable combination of a magical sneeze, a dirty handkerchief, and the kind of desperate courage that comes from having absolutely nothing to lose.

Since becoming king, Scab has proven sharper than anyone expected. He is cunning, ruthless when he needs to be, and fiercely protective of the Society that once tormented him. ## APPENDIX S: THE SNOT PIT

The following locations are available for the players to explore before departing on their mission.

### Scab's Throne

In the center of the Snot Pit, an extra-high heap of compacted trash rises above the surrounding shacks. At its peak sits a crude war tent - stitched rat hides stretched over a frame of bent pipe and scrap wood. Inside, King Scab's throne - a mass of garbage and scrap metal bolted together with stolen rivets - faces the tent's entrance. The Pole of Stinky rises from the top of the heap just outside the tent flap, the sacred snotrag flapping in the sewer draft, visible from everywhere in the basin. Scab's advisors and a handful of guards are the only ones allowed up the heap. Hangers-on, petitioners, and gossips are shooed away - Scab doesn't have the patience for them.

### The Bunks

A series of wide connecting pipes along the island's eastern edge, converted into sleeping quarters. Rat-hide hammocks are slung between pipe walls, stacked three high. Personal belongings hang from hooks made of bent nails. The Bunks are noisy, cramped, and smell like sleeping goblin under the ever-present aroma of Stinky, but they're dry and defensible - only two entrances, both guarded.

### The Brew Pipe

A dead-end section of pipe jutting from the island's northern edge, converted into the Snot Pit's kitchen and brewery. **Old Gusset**, a one-eyed goblin cook, runs the operation with an iron ladle and a foul temper. The Brew Pipe produces Toadphlegm Soup (rat meat boiled in dubious broth) and fermented mushroom wine (tastes like dirt and regret but gets the job done). A large cauldron simmers perpetually. Goblins come and go with clay bowls throughout the day.

Old Gusset knows everything that happens in the Snot Pit. Extracting gossip from him requires either flattering his cooking (DC 8 Persuasion) or enduring a full bowl of his latest experimental recipe (DC 10 Constitution save or be poisoned for 10 minutes).

### The Scrap Market

An open area near the center of the trash island where Snotrockets barter, trade, and steal from each other. Blankets spread on the ground display salvaged goods: bent cutlery, bits of wire, interesting rocks, dead beetles in jars, scraps of surface-world fabric, and the occasional genuinely useful item. Prices are negotiated in copper pieces, favors, and threats.

A character spending 30 minutes browsing the Scrap Market can find basic adventuring supplies (rope, torches, rations) at roughly double normal price. A DC 14 Investigation check uncovers one unusual item (DM's choice - a potion, a scroll, a surprisingly good dagger, etc.).

### The Stink Shrine

A small sheltered alcove on the island's southern edge, maintained by **Nosebleed Peg**, the Snot Pit's self-appointed priestess of Stinky. The alcove is decorated with crude drawings of Stinky performing miracles - curing diseases, smiting enemies, making flowers grow (Peg's theology is creative). Small offerings sit at the base of a miniature replica of the Pole of Stinky: rat bones, shiny pebbles, dried mushrooms, and the occasional copper piece.

Nosebleed Peg is happy to bless the Filthy Few before their mission. Her blessing involves smearing sacred fungus paste on each goblin's forehead while chanting in a language she invented. The blessing has no mechanical effect, but Peg insists it provides "the spiritual protection of the Great Snotrag." Refusing the blessing visibly upsets her.

### The Docks

Along the island's western edge, a stretch of waterlogged planking serves as the berth for the Snotrocket navy. A dozen or so vessels are moored here at any given time - mostly stolen wooden washtubs fitted with crude spring-loaded catapult launchers for lobbing bewilderment bombs, alongside barrel rafts lashed together from empty Ratpiss Reserve casks. The fleet is commanded by **Admiral Pudge**, a barrel-chested goblin who wears a stolen child's tricorn hat and takes his rank with absolute seriousness despite the fact that his flagship is a bathtub. The navy patrols the dark water around the island, fishes for drain fish, and ferries goblins to and from the basin walls where the tunnel entrances are.

### Gunk's Bridge

Gunk the troll lives under a junction bridge where the basin wall meets the western drainage tunnels, at the border between Snotrocket territory and the unclaimed Blackflow. See **Recruiting Gunk** in the main adventure.

### Fizzpop's Closet

Fizzpop the tinker operates out of a sealed maintenance closet accessible via a narrow service tunnel on the basin's eastern side. See **Speaking with Fizzpop** in the main adventure.
